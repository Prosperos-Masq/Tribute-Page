Morgan Freeman

Momentous Moments in Freeman's Life

  • Morgan Freeman was born on June 1, 1937 in Memphis, Tennessee.

  • Freeman made his acting debut at age nine, playing the lead role in a school play.

  • At age 12, he won a statewide drama competition.

  • served as an Automatic Tracking Radar Repairman, rising to the rank of Airman 1st Class.

  • Freeman first became known on the soap opera "Another World" and "The Electric Company".

  • He received the Obie Award in 1980 for the title role in Coriolanus.

  • Freeman, together with Lori McCreary, founded the film production company Revelations Entertainment.

  • Freeman also hosts the channel Our Space on ClickStar, with specially crafted film clips in which he shares his love for the sciences, especially space exploration and aeronautics

  • he won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his performance in Million Dollar Baby

  • In 2005 alone, he provided narration for two films, War of the Worlds and the Academy Award-winning documentary film March of the Penguins.

  • In July 2009, Freeman was one of the presenters at the 46664 Concert celebrating Nelson Mandela's birthday at Radio City Music Hall in New York City.
